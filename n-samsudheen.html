About

N. Samsudheen is a senior Indian Union Muslim League (IUML) leader and four-time MLA representing the Mannarkkad constituency in Palakkad district. Born on 31 May 1969 in Murivazhikkal, Paravanna, Tirur, Kerala, he pursued his education at Thunchan Memorial Government College, Pocker Sahib Memorial Orphanage College, and Government Law College, Kozhikode. A recipient of the prestigious K.K. Nair Memorial Shreshtha Legislative Assembly Social Award for outstanding Assembly work, Samsudheen is known for his meticulous approach, gentle leadership, and powerful oratory. In 2026, he was appointed Minister for General Education in the UDF government led by Chief Minister V. D. Satheesan — a role the IUML leadership felt he was uniquely suited for.

Championed inclusive education policies including student-welfare initiatives and the landmark proposal for menstrual leave for schoolgirls to support student wellbeing.

Grassroots Leadership

Led Mannarkkad constituency for 15 years across both sides of the aisle, building an unmatched reputation for accessibility, accountability, and community engagement.
